Macaque Brainnetome Atlas

Macaque Brainnetome Atlas (MBA) is a new macaque monkey atlas characterized by the anatomical connectivity of 304 subregions accompanied with the structural and the resting functional connectome. Compared with the existing monkey brain atlas, our atlas has several advantages: 1) the population atlas uses high spatial and angular resolution DWI images of 8 ex-vivo monkey samples to present the reproducible connectivity contrast; 2) it conveys both detailed structural and resting functional connection pattern of each subregion; 3) A comprehensive validation was conducted across different dataset including ex-vivo, in-vivo MRI images and tracer study; 4) white surface (white/gray matter interface) based tracking was used for cortical parcellation to avoid streamlines originating from gray matter where the orientation estimation uncertainty increases; 5) we provide both surface and volume versions of the atlas for convenient and extensive usage.

Brainnetome Macaque PM Dataset

Brainnetome macaque PM dataset includes post-mortem diffusion data from a 9.4T scanner on eight macaques.

Scanner Specifications

  • Scanner type: 9.4T Bruker scanner (Bruker Biospec 94/30 USR, Ettlingen, Baden-Wurttemberg)
  • Coil: a 72 mm inner-diameter quadrature birdcage RF coil (Bruker Biospec, Ettlingen, Baden-Wurttemberg)

Species

Macaca mulatta

Sample Description

  • Sample size: 8
  • Age distribution: 4-7 years (mean 5.6, std 1.06)
  • Sex distribution: n=2 male, n=6 female

Click here for the full sample description (.csv)

Scan sequences

Diffusion-weighted

  • 3D spin-echo EPI diffusion weighted sequence
  • Matrix size: 148×120×160
  • Resolution: 0.45×0.45×0.45 mm3
  • FOV: 66.6×54.0×72.0 mm
  • TE: 25ms
  • TR: 200ms
  • Multiple shells: 6 b = 0 s/mm2, 60 b = 2400 s/mm2, 120 b=4800 s/mm2

T2-weighted

  • 3D MSME sequence
  • Matrix size: 240×180×267
  • Resolution: 0.3×0.3×0.3 mm3
  • FOV: 72.0×54.0×80.1mm
  • TE: 36 ms
  • TR: 2000 ms
